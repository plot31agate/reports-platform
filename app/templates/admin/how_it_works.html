How reports work

Where the data comes from, how every stat is derived, and what the AI writes. Reference for the team.

The pipeline

Pull or upload data→ Parse & normalise→ AI scores & writes→ Review & edit→ Publish HTML + PDF

Every source lands as a file in data/{client}/{period}/, named {source}_{period}.csv. API syncs and manual uploads produce identical files, so a month can mix both freely. Building a report parses whatever is in that folder — a missing source simply drops its section from the published report.

Where the data comes from

SourceProviderHow it arrives
Media mentions RSS / Atom feeds Auto-fetch on the workspace reads the client's mention feeds — Google Alerts for the brand and executives, plus any publication RSS or alert-platform feed added under "Mention feeds" — and writes the mentions CSV. Alert feeds only keep the ~20 most recent items, so historic months are compiled from the Gmail alerts archive. Duplicate stories are removed by matching URL and title.
Backlinks & authority Ahrefs API One agency-level API key (API keys page). Pulls the backlink snapshot (referring domains, total backlinks, top linking sites) and 12 monthly history points for domain rating, referring domains and estimated organic traffic.
Search performance Google Search Console API, or Ahrefs GSC Insights Clicks, impressions, CTR, position and top queries. Two routes: set the Search Console property on the client's Google card and the data comes straight from Google (the service account email must be added as a user in Search Console — no Ahrefs project needed); or leave that blank and it reads GSC through the client's Ahrefs project instead (the project must have GSC connected — set the project ID on the Ahrefs card).
Core keywords Ahrefs Site Explorer The keywords the client is judged on, listed on their Ahrefs card. Each month pulls volume, market and position for the target domain, alongside last month's position from the same call, so the report can show the move and call out what broke into the top five and what fell furthest. Nothing needs adding to Rank Tracker — a keyword the site does not rank for is reported as unranked. Leave the list blank to report the domain's 25 highest-volume keywords instead.
Competitor benchmark Ahrefs API Competitors are read from the client's Ahrefs rank-tracker project. Each brand's domain rating, referring domains and estimated monthly organic traffic are pulled for the share-of-voice section.
Technical SEO Ahrefs Site Audit + our register The site health score and domain rating come from Ahrefs Site Audit each month. The findings themselves live in our issue register CSV, maintained by us with severity, status and timeframe per finding.
Site traffic Google Analytics 4 A Google service account (agency-level JSON key) reads the client's GA4 property: sessions, users, engaged sessions, top pages, channels, and sessions by country for the geography section. The service account email needs Viewer access on the property.
LinkedIn Manual export The monthly XLSX export from LinkedIn company analytics, dropped on the workspace card: followers, new followers, post impressions, engagements and top posts.

How the stats are derived

StatDerivation
Month on month This month's headline numbers are compared against the same numbers parsed fresh from the previous month's data folder. Chips show the percentage change; a metric with no prior data shows "New baseline". The first tracked month is presented as the baseline.
Sentiment Claude reads every mention's title and snippet with the client's context brief and classifies it positive, neutral or negative with a score. The net score is the average across all scored mentions, from −1 to +1. Scores are cached per mention, so rebuilds never re-score or change history.
Share of voice Each brand's Ahrefs estimated monthly organic search traffic as a share of the tracked set's combined total. It measures search visibility, not media coverage. The client's share change vs the previous month is shown once two months are tracked.
Executive visibility The month's mentions are scanned for the executive names configured on the client. A hit in a story's title or snippet counts as a mention for that person.
Authority trends Twelve monthly Ahrefs snapshots drawn as sparklines. The delta compares the latest month against twelve months ago.
Site health Ahrefs Site Audit health score out of 100, with the change vs the prior month. Open findings are register entries with no resolved month, ordered High → Medium → Low; High and Medium appear in the report table, Low as a count.
Search & traffic Sums and averages over the export rows: total clicks and impressions, average CTR and position from GSC data; sessions, users and engaged sessions from GA4. Top queries and top pages are ranked by clicks and views.

What the AI writes

After parsing, the full month's data summary is passed to Claude, which writes the report's editorial layer:

  • Headline and standfirst — the cover title naming the month's defining story, and the one-to-two sentence frame beneath it.
  • The month in brief — the executive summary at the top of the report: what defined the month, how coverage, search, traffic, authority and social connect, and where the focus goes next.
  • Section commentary — a short story-first intro under each section heading, tying that section's numbers to what drove them.
  • What worked, what to watch — the month's wins with the numbers behind them, and the soft spots worth an eye.
  • Next month — recommended actions in three buckets: lean into, investigate, fix urgently, each with the reasoning.

The first build seeds all of this into the review screen. Anything you edit there is saved and always wins — later rebuilds only fill in fields left blank, and never overwrite your words. Sentiment uses a fast model per mention; the writing uses a stronger model once per build.

Review and publishing

  1. Sync or upload the month's sources on the client workspace — each card parses immediately and shows a status.
  2. Build the report. Parsing, sentiment, AI writing and rendering run in the background.
  3. Open Review to read and edit every piece of commentary in place, then "Save and regenerate".
  4. The published report is a web page (/c/{client}/{period}) and a print-grade A4 PDF rendered from the same page. Clients reach it through their portal login or a share link, and every view is recorded.
